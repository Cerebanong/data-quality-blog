| Data quality work predates the term | 003 — Why I Am Writing This Blog | People have been doing data quality work — capturing, organizing, and validating information so others can trust it — long before 'data quality' became a named discipline. The practice is older than the profession. |

Absent from vendor and academic data quality taxonomies, which organize by symptom type or system layer. |
| Audit analogy for governance | 006 — What Is Data Governance? | Data governance is to data management as audit is to finance — it provides oversight, accountability, and standards over operational work, not another layer of bureaucracy. |

| Transactional GIGO | 009 — The Two Types of Garbage In, Garbage Out | A subtype of garbage in, garbage out where data enters the system already corrupted -- through flawed processes, fraudulent documentation, or broken incentives -- and every downstream consumer inherits the garbage. The fix is prevention: input validation, process governance, and shift-left quality checks at the point of data creation. |
| Structural GIGO | 009 — The Two Types of Garbage In, Garbage Out | A subtype of garbage in, garbage out where data was accurate when captured but the reality it describes changed, causing the data to age into garbage. The fix is not prevention but continuous revalidation: refresh cadences, decay monitoring, and shelf-life policies tied to how fast the underlying reality changes. |
